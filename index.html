Research Interests

My research has focused on the design and security analysis of symmetric-key cryptography, particularly against practical implementation attacks such as side-channel and fault attacks. My work focuses on designing and analyzing cryptosystems that remain secure under realistic attack models.

Looking ahead, I aim to expand my research to broader areas of cryptography, including post-quantum cryptography, modes of operation, zero-knowledge proofs, and blockchain-related protocols. I am interested in both the design and security analysis of these systems, with an emphasis on practical and implementation-aware security.
